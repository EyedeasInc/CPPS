About CPPS

At the Canadian Power Platform Summit, our mission is to empower individuals and organizations through the innovative use of Microsoft's Power Platform. As a non-profit organization, we strive to create an inclusive and collaborative environment where attendees can learn, share, and grow their skills. By bringing together a diverse community of professionals, we aim to foster knowledge exchange, inspire creativity, and drive digital transformation across Canada.

The inaugural Canadian Power Platform Summit (CPPS) was an absolute hit, eh! With a fantastic turnout, attendees from across Canada and beyond dove into insightful sessions, hands-on workshops, and some epic networking. The vibe was incredible, filled with excitement and energy as participants exchanged ideas and built lasting connections. We had expert speakers who shared their invaluable knowledge, making the sessions both educational and inspiring.

Building on this amazing success, we’re even more pumped for the next one. Set for March 20-21, 2026, right before the Microsoft MVP Summit, we’re gearing up to welcome top-notch international speakers and create another unforgettable experience in beautiful Vancouver.

Who should attend CPPS?

CPPS is ideal for anyone passionate about leveraging the Microsoft Power Platform to drive business transformation and innovation. This includes business analysts, developers, IT professionals, data scientists, and decision-makers across various industries.

What can I expect at CPPS?

Attendees can expect a dynamic mix of insightful sessions, hands-on workshops, and networking opportunities. They'll have the chance to learn from expert speakers, gain practical experience with Power Platform tools, and connect with like-minded professionals to exchange ideas and best practices.

Where will CPPS 2026 be held?

CPPS 2026 will take place in the vibrant British Columbia, at the Microsoft Vancouver office, located at 725 Granville St, Vancouver, BC V7Y 1G5. Vancouver is known for its stunning natural scenery, diverse culture, and thriving tech scene.

Why are you charging $299 for the full-day workshops?

The $299 fee helps cover the costs of organizing and delivering a high-quality workshop, including venue expenses, materials, and other logistical needs. Any revenue generated beyond these costs will go directly toward providing an honorarium to the trainers and content creators as a token of appreciation for their time, expertise, and effort in developing and delivering the workshop. This fee ensures we can offer a professional and enriching experience while recognizing the value of the trainers’ contributions.

What is the weather like in Vancouver in March?

In March, Vancouver experiences mild temperatures, with average highs around 10°C (50°F) and lows around 4°C (39°F). While it can be rainy at times, the city's temperate climate makes it an enjoyable destination for visitors.
